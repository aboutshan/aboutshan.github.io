Twisted Bilayer Graphene via Hetero-site Nucleation

When scientists twist single atomic layers of suitable materials, such as graphene, new properties emerge. It was shown in 2018 that as the twist angle between two graphene sheets is tuned to about 1°, the physical properties of the system change dramatically.

Our work (paper on Nat. Comms. and patent)

My contribution: experiment procedure determination, (automatic) twisted angle statistics, patent writing

We devised an innovative methodology to synthesize twisted bilayer or few-layer graphene, which has a non-trivial twisting angle between layers, based on chemical vapor deposition (CVD).

Traditionally when we implement CVD to grow graphene, due to interlayer coupling effect, the newly formed layer tends to remain aligned with the previous one, forming a typical AB stacking order.

To reduce such effect, we introduced perturbation during the growing process, successfully leading the nucleation site of the second layer graphene to deviate from that of the first layer. The non-trivial twist angles were examined by later characterization. The wide range of twisted angles provides convenience for later research into this area.


Advisors: Zhongfan Liu, Hailing Peng

Molecular Beam Epitaxy

In Prof.Jian Wang's lab, the ultrahigh-vacuum MBE-STM combined system (Scienta Omicron) enables us to make in situ measurements of the sample grown by molecular-beam epitaxy technique.

One of the intensively studied systems is the one-unit-cell FeSe/SrTiO3(001), with or without Fe, Pb adatoms or clusters. The Nb-doped SrTiO3(001) substrate was pretreated by the Se-flux method in MBE chamber. Then the 1-UC FeSe film was grown by coevaporating high-purity Fe (99.994%) and Se (99.999%) from an e-beam evaporator and a Knudsen cell, respectively, at a substrate temperature of 400 °C, followed by annealing at 450 °C for hours. After the preparation of the 1-UC FeSe sample, we could deposit magnetic or nonmagnetic atoms on the FeSe surface.

Besides, by flash annealing in UHV, we can prepare large-scale bilayer graphene on nominally flat 6H-SiC(0001) substrates.
